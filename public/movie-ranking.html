Top 3 movies of all time

Bommarillu

Romantic drama movie

*Bommarillu* (2006) is a heartwarming Telugu romantic drama directed by Bhaskar, known for its emotional depth and relatable themes of family, love, and individuality. The story revolves around Siddharth (played by Siddharth), a young man who feels stifled by his overbearing yet loving father, Aravind (Prakash Raj). Aravind meticulously plans every detail of Siddharth's life, from his career to his future wife, leaving Siddharth with no space to make his own choices.

When Siddharth secretly falls in love with Hasini (Genelia D’Souza), a cheerful and carefree girl, his world turns upside down. Hasini’s lively personality and her honest, independent outlook on life captivate Siddharth, offering him the freedom and happiness he craves. However, introducing Hasini to his traditional family leads to conflicts, as their contrasting personalities and values clash.

The film beautifully portrays Siddharth's journey of self-discovery as he learns to balance his love for Hasini and his loyalty to his family. With heartwarming moments, emotional confrontations, and a perfect blend of humor and romance, *Bommarillu* highlights the importance of communication, understanding, and individuality within familial bonds.

Ye Jawani Hai Dewani

Young adult movie

*Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani* (2013), directed by Ayan Mukerji, is a Bollywood coming-of-age film that beautifully captures the essence of friendship, love, dreams, and self-discovery. The story follows four friends—Kabir "Bunny" (Ranbir Kapoor), Naina (Deepika Padukone), Aditi (Kalki Koechlin), and Avi (Aditya Roy Kapur)—whose lives intertwine during a trekking trip to Manali. Naina, a shy and studious medical student, joins the trip on a whim, and it becomes a life-changing experience for her as she discovers the joy of living in the moment and falls for Bunny’s carefree and adventurous spirit.

Bunny, a free-spirited wanderer, dreams of traveling the world and refuses to settle down. His ambitions create a distance between him and his friends, especially Naina, who silently nurses her feelings for him. Eight years later, the group reunites for Aditi’s wedding, and old friendships are rekindled. Bunny and Naina’s chemistry resurfaces, but their contrasting aspirations and lifestyles pose challenges to their relationship.

The film beautifully balances humor, drama, and romance, with a strong emphasis on personal growth and the importance of cherishing relationships. With stunning visuals, soulful music, and relatable characters, *Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani* is a celebration of youth, love, and the bittersweet journey of growing up.

Your Name

Romantic movie

*Your Name* (*Kimi no Na wa.*) is a 2016 Japanese animated romantic fantasy film directed by Makoto Shinkai. The story revolves around two teenagers, Taki, a high school boy from Tokyo, and Mitsuha, a girl from a rural town called Itomori, who inexplicably begin to swap bodies. They wake up in each other’s lives, learning to navigate their contrasting worlds while leaving notes and messages to communicate.

As they grow closer, Taki and Mitsuha develop a deep bond, despite never having met in person. However, the body-swapping phenomenon suddenly stops, and Taki sets out to find Mitsuha, uncovering a shocking truth about a catastrophic event that occurred in her town three years earlier. Determined to save her and her community, Taki takes extraordinary steps to change their fates.

With its breathtaking animation, emotional depth, and a moving exploration of time, love, and destiny, *Your Name* became a global sensation, resonating with audiences worldwide. It’s a masterpiece that artfully blends romance, fantasy, and heartbreak, leaving a lasting impression on viewers.
